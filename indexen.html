Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) Risk Assessment Test
"Understanding Symptoms - Protecting Legs - Preventing Pulmonary Complications"

Instruction: Answer Yes/No to the following questions.

1. Active cancer
(Treatment or palliation within 6 months)
2. Paralysis, paresis, or recent plaster immobilization of the lower extremity
3. Bedridden recently >3 days or major
(surgery within 12 weeks)
4. Localized tenderness along the deep venous system
5. Entire leg swollen
6. Calf swelling >3 cm compared to the other leg
(Measured 10 cm below tibial tuberosity)
7. Pitting edema, confined to symptomatic leg
8. Collateral (nonvaricose) superficial veins present
9. Previously documented DVT
10. Alternative diagnosis to DVT as likely or more likely
